Funds-Axis

I worked with Funds-Axis over a period of 4 months to create a variety of digital and print assets depending on the business needs.

As the only designer on the team, I had control over the look and feel of the work produced, but it was important to ensure they fit within the company guidelines, and portrayed a professional feel.

Brochure Design

Funds-Axis offers their clients a bunch of brochures and white papers with information on their product. The team wanted me to create a standard template which would have a consistent look across all brochures; something which would be recognisable as belonging to Funds-Axis.
I used the brand colours and sharp, angular shapes which made up the logo as a guideline for how each element should be styled.
Some of these brochures were up to 50 pages long, so I used icons and boxes to highlight key texts, breaking up the content and making it easier to digest for the reader.

Icon Design

I designed a series of icons to be used on Funds-Axis website to help categorise the information, for example; Papers, Reporting, Forms and Acts.

They had some existing icons which were simple line illustrations. I decided to update them to use colour fills and the illusion of negative spacing to add more dimension and depth to the icons, making them look more visually interesting.

Wiki Design

Funds-Axis has an internal 'Wiki' website, where their customers can access a wealth of information.
As this would be edited frequently by product owners, I created a style guide to ensure the content was consistent. This involved outlining colours, typography, and layout, as well as how to use imagery, tables and icons.
I had to consider the vast amount of information when designing each page of the Wiki, where there is too much information, a user will become overwhelmed and struggle to find specific parts. Therefore I broke the text up with tables, used diagrams and imagery where possible and worked closely on the information architecture to keep it easy to navigate through.

Explainer Illustrations

Oftentimes the website and brochures would be a guide for the user when working with the product. So, to make the information easier to understand, I created various illustrations which helped explain the content.

These two illustrations represent uploading data to the cloud, and selecting a report. To create these I stuck to the company colour palette and experimented with lines to add definition to each element.
